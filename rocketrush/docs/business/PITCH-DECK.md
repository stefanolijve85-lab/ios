## Slide 1 — Title
- **LiftoffX** — *The crash game operators actually earn from.*
- One line: "Aviator-class instant game with a built-in social layer and a
  revenue model that gives operators more margin."
- Live demo link + your contact.

## Slide 2 — The problem (for operators)
- Instant/crash games are now a **top-3 revenue category**, but supply is
  concentrated in a few providers (Spribe/Aviator, etc.).
- Operators face: **high fixed rev-share**, **fixed RTP** they can't tune to
  their market, **generic UX**, and **little differentiation** vs every other
  casino running the exact same game.
- Result: a hit game, but thin operator margin and zero brand ownership.

## Slide 3 — The solution
- **LiftoffX**: a provably-fair crash game that is
  1. **Aviator-class** on gameplay & feel (dual bets, auto-cashout, live rounds),
  2. **Social by design** — leaderboards, live activity feed, profiles, chat →
     higher engagement & retention (more rounds = more GGR),
  3. **Operator-friendly** — configurable RTP, better rev-share, white-label skin.

## Slide 4 — Product / demo
- Screenshots + **live link** (the MVP).
- Highlights: shared server-authoritative rounds, **provably fair** (player can
  verify any round), **dual simultaneous bets**, auto-cashout, multi-language,
  mobile-first PWA, live players + activity feed.
- "Everyone who opens it is in the *same* live round" — true multiplayer.

## Slide 5 — Why now
- Instant/crash games went from niche to mainstream post-Aviator; double-digit
  YoY growth in emerging + regulated markets.
- Operators are **actively seeking alternatives** to diversify away from a single
  dominant supplier and to own more of the player relationship.

## Slide 6 — Differentiation (vs the incumbent)
| | Incumbent (Aviator-style) | **LiftoffX** |
|---|---|---|
| RTP | Fixed (≈97%) | **Configurable** (97/96/95%) per market |
| Rev-share | High, flat (~15%) | **Lower + volume-tiered** |
| Retention | Basic | **Social layer** (leaderboard, feed, profiles) |
| Branding | Provider-branded | **White-label / "powered by operator"** |
| Fairness | Provably fair | Provably fair **+ certified-RNG roadmap** |

## Slide 7 — Business model & operator economics
- Model: **% of GGR** (volume-tiered), or flat-fee option for high-volume.
- Headline: *at the same player base, the operator keeps more under LiftoffX.*
- Pull 2–3 numbers straight from `OPERATOR-ECONOMICS.md` (e.g. "per 1,000 active
  players/month the operator keeps **€X more** than under a 15% incumbent deal").
- Show the tiered staffel + the "better-for-operator" comparison table.

## Slide 8 — Go-to-market
- **You don't integrate 5,000 operators one by one.** Distribution = aggregators.
- Phase 1: certify + list on **2–3 aggregators** (EveryMatrix/Hub88, SoftSwiss,
  Slotegrator, Pariplay, Oryx/Bragg) → instant reach to thousands of operators.
- Phase 2: direct deals with tier-1/2 operators who want a custom skin.
- Phase 3: branded tournaments/jackpots network across operators.

## Slide 9 — Traction & roadmap
- **Now:** MVP live & shareable (provably fair, social, dual bets, multiplayer).
- **Next:** RGS + seamless wallet API → certification (GLI/iTech) → B2B license →
  first aggregator listing → first operators live.
- Show a dated timeline; mark the licensing/cert milestones explicitly.

## Slide 10 — Compliance & tech
- Architecture: **RGS (Remote Game Server)** + thin client; **seamless wallet API**
  (operator holds the balance).
- Provably fair today; **certified RNG (GLI-19/GLI-11)** for regulated markets.
- Responsible-gaming hooks, audit logs, reporting, multi-currency, HA/low-latency.

## Slide 11 — The ask
- What you're raising/seeking and what it unlocks:
  - Capital for **licensing + certification** (the long pole), or
  - A **partnership/JV** with an already-licensed studio to shortcut it.
- Be explicit: amount, use of funds, milestones it buys.
