## 2. Find the *right person* (don't guess emails)

Never invent an address. Two reliable ways in:

1. **The official form** — most have "**Become a partner / provider**" or "Content
   submission". Submitting it is a legitimate, expected entry point. Use template **A**.
2. **LinkedIn** — search the company + one of these titles:
   - Head of / Director of **Partnerships**
   - Head of **Content** / **Game Aggregation** / **Studio Relations**
   - **Commercial** / **BD** Director, **Chief Commercial Officer**
   - For Tier-1: **Studio Onboarding** / **Partner Studios** lead

> If you find a likely email pattern (e.g. firstname.lastname@company.com) you can
> verify it with a free checker, but **LinkedIn + the form is enough** — don't spray
> guessed addresses (hurts deliverability and your name).

## 5. The demo call (≈5 min demo + research)

**Mindset: half pitch, half market research.** Every call should leave you with
real numbers (rev-share, certs, wallet, licence) — see [`OPERATOR-CALL-QUESTIONS.md`](./OPERATOR-CALL-QUESTIONS.md).

| Time | Do / say |
|---|---|
| 0:00 | **Frame:** "Aviator-class crash + a social layer + better operator economics. I'll show it live in 2 min, then I'd love your read on fit and what you'd need from us." |
| 0:30 | **Demo live** (share www.liftoffx.com): place **two bets**, **cash one out early, ride the other**, hit the **win pop-up**. Call out **provably fair** (tap to verify), **live players still in the round**, **leaderboard + activity feed**. |
| 2:30 | **Pitch:** configurable RTP · **volume-tiered rev-share (operators keep more)** · white-label/"powered by operator". "We don't just split the pie cheaper — the social layer grows it." |
| 3:30 | **Ask + learn:** "Does this fit your hub? What **certs/wallet** would you need? What **rev-share** is standard for crash content?" |
| 4:30 | **Next step:** intro to their integration team, or a follow-up with the **math/RTP sheet**. Agree the specific next action before you hang up. |

## 6. Objection handling (have these ready)
- **"We already have Aviator/Spribe."** → Exactly why this is interesting — operators
  want a **second** crash option to diversify supplier risk and keep more margin. We're
  the differentiated alternative, not a clone.
- **"Are you certified / licensed?"** → Provably fair today; clear certification roadmap
  (GLI/iTech). We'd **prioritise the markets and certs you need**, and we're open to a
  **Powered-By / white-label** route to shortcut it. *(This is the question that tells
  you which funding route is real — write the answer down.)*
- **"What's your traction?"** → MVP is **live with real multiplayer + a social layer**.
  We're selecting **launch partners now** — early partners get roadmap input and
  preferential terms.
- **"Why would players switch?"** → They don't switch *games* — the **social layer**
  (leaderboards, activity, tournaments) lifts session length and retention vs. a plain
  crash game. Upside for the operator at the same hold.
- **"Rev-share?"** → Volume-tiered (e.g. 14% → 10% → 7%), flat-fee option at scale.
  "Tell me what's market for you and we'll make it work." *(Then record their number.)*
- **"Send me info."** → "Will do — one-pager + math sheet today. Can I also grab 15
  min next week to get your read on fit?" *(Always convert to a meeting.)*

## 8. 14-day run sheet (so you actually do it)

- **Day 1–2:** Setup (§0). Pick 8 targets. Find the right person for each (§2).
- **Day 2–3:** Send Touch 1 (LinkedIn connect **C** + form/email **A**) to all 8.
- **Day 4–5:** Follow-up #1 (**B1**) to non-repliers; DM (**D**) anyone who connected.
- **Day 6–8:** Run any calls that come in (§5). Log every answer in the tracker.
- **Day 9–10:** Follow-up #2 (**B2**, the math-sheet angle).
- **Day 11–13:** Breakup (**E**) to silent ones. Book remaining calls.
- **Day 14:** Review the tracker. Update [`OPERATOR-ECONOMICS.md`](./OPERATOR-ECONOMICS.md)
  + [`FUNDING.md`](./FUNDING.md) with the **real** rev-share / cert / wallet numbers.
  Decide route (JV vs own studio) on the evidence — that's Phase 4.

---

## 9. Two rules that make or break it
1. **Always convert to a meeting.** Info-sends without a meeting ask die. Every reply →
   "great, 15 min next week?"
2. **Every call is research.** You're not just selling — you're collecting the rev-share,
   cert, wallet and licence numbers that decide your whole funding path. Write them down.

> ⚠️ Liftoff X is **provably fair but not yet certified/licensed** — never claim
> otherwise. The honest framing ("clear roadmap, we'll prioritise your markets") is a
> strength, not a weakness, in these calls.
